Milano Through Time

Journey through the four great eras that shaped Milan's magnificent heritage

222 BC - 476 AD

Roman Era

Mediolanum - The Western Capital

Milan's story begins as Mediolanum, founded by the Celtic Insubres and conquered by the Romans in 222 BC. Under Roman rule, the city flourished as a major commercial and military center. In 286 AD, Emperor Diocletian made Mediolanum the capital of the Western Roman Empire, establishing it as one of the most important cities in the ancient world. The famous Edict of Milan, issued by Emperor Constantine in 313 AD, granted religious tolerance throughout the empire, marking a pivotal moment in Christian history.

Key Events

  • 222 BC - Roman conquest of Celtic Mediolanum
  • 286 AD - Becomes capital of Western Roman Empire
  • 313 AD - Edict of Milan grants religious freedom
  • 374 AD - St. Ambrose becomes bishop
  • 402 AD - Imperial court moves to Ravenna
1386 - 1500 AD

Gothic Period

The Cathedral and Medieval Splendor

The Gothic period witnessed Milan's transformation into a medieval powerhouse under the Visconti family. The most iconic achievement of this era was the beginning of the Duomo construction in 1386, a masterpiece of Gothic architecture that would take nearly six centuries to complete. The city became a center of trade and craftsmanship, with magnificent palaces and churches rising throughout the urban landscape. The Visconti and later the Sforza families established Milan as one of Italy's most powerful city-states.

Key Events

  • 1277 - Visconti family rises to power
  • 1386 - Construction of the Duomo begins
  • 1395 - Gian Galeazzo Visconti becomes Duke
  • 1450 - Sforza family takes control
  • 1492 - La Scala della Ragione completed
1450 - 1600 AD

Renaissance

Leonardo's Milan and Artistic Flowering

The Renaissance brought unprecedented cultural flourishing to Milan under the Sforza dynasty. The court of Ludovico Sforza attracted the greatest artists of the age, most notably Leonardo da Vinci, who spent nearly two decades in the city (1482-1499). During this period, Leonardo created masterpieces including "The Last Supper" at Santa Maria delle Grazie and designed innovative engineering projects. The Sforza Castle was expanded and beautified, becoming one of Europe's most magnificent ducal residences and a center of Renaissance learning and art.

Key Events

  • 1450 - Francesco Sforza becomes Duke of Milan
  • 1482 - Leonardo da Vinci arrives in Milan
  • 1495-1498 - Creation of "The Last Supper"
  • 1499 - French invasion ends Sforza rule
  • 1535 - Spanish rule begins
1800 - Present

Modern Milan

Industrial Capital to Fashion Metropolis

Modern Milan emerged as Italy's economic powerhouse during the Industrial Revolution. The opening of La Scala in 1778 established the city's cultural prestige, while the 19th century brought railways, factories, and urban expansion. The 20th century saw Milan's transformation into the global fashion and design capital we know today, hosting major international events like Expo 2015 and preparing for the 2026 Winter Olympics. Today's Milan brilliantly balances its rich historical heritage with cutting-edge innovation and contemporary architecture.

Key Events

  • 1778 - La Scala opera house opens
  • 1861 - Milan joins the Kingdom of Italy
  • 1946 - Birth of Italian fashion industry
  • 2015 - Milan Expo attracts 21 million visitors
  • 2026 - Milan-Cortina Winter Olympics
